Past Simple Passive

Structure: was/were + past participle (V3)

Affirmative:
The first aeroplane was flown by the Wright Brothers.
Television was invented in 1927.

Negative:
The telephone wasn't made in the 20th century.

When to use:

  • Action completed in the past
  • Don't know/not important who did it
  • Focus on the invention, not inventor

Was vs Were

Subject Use Example
I, he, she, it (singular) was It was invented
you, we, they (plural) were They were made

Passive Questions

Yes/No questions: Was/Were + S + V3?

Was television invented in 1927?
Were films made in the UK?

WH-questions:

Question word Structure Example
Who Who was S V3 by? Who was it invented by?
When When was S V3? When was it invented?
Where Where was S V3? Where was it made?
What What was V3...? What was invented in 1876?
