Structure:

  • Affirmative: S + will + V (base)
  • Negative: S + won't (will not) + V (base)
  • Question: Will + S + V (base)?
The streets will be cleaner.
We won't drive big cars.
Will we do anything differently? - Yes, we will. / No, we won't.

  • Question: Am/Is/Are + S + V-ing?
We're going on holiday next week.
We aren't flying. We're travelling by train.
Are you travelling by train?

When to Use Will vs Present Continuous?

Use WILL for:

  • Predictions about the future
  • Instant decisions
  • Things we think/believe will happen

Use PRESENT CONTINUOUS for:

  • Arranged plans (already decided)
  • Fixed appointments
  • Things we've prepared for
Will: I think it will rain. (prediction)
Present Continuous: I'm meeting Tom at 5pm. (arranged)
