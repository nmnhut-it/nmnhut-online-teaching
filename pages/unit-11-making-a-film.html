Active vs Passive Voice

Active: Subject does the action

Passive: Subject receives the action

Active: They make films in London.
Passive: Films are made in London.

When to use passive:

  • Don't know who does the action
  • Not important who does it
  • Want to focus on the action/object

Present Simple Passive

Structure: am/is/are + past participle (V3)

Affirmative:
French is spoken here.
Films are made in Hollywood.

Negative:
English isn't spoken here.

Question:
Is French spoken here?

Past Participle (V3)

Regular verbs: add -ed (same as past simple)

Irregular verbs: learn the third form

Base Past Past Participle
speak spoke spoken
make made made
write wrote written

BY vs WITH

BY = Who does the action (person/agent)

The film is directed by Spielberg.
The food is brought by a waiter.

WITH = Tool/material/instrument used

Bread is cut with a knife.
The streets are decorated with lights.
The picture is painted with oil paints.

Both together:

The picture was painted by Picasso with oil paints.
